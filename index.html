Brendan's Film Recommendations

Barry Lyndon

Written and Directed by Stanley Kubrick

Stanley Kubrick is known for many films such as, The Shining, 2001: A Space Odyssey, and Clockwork Orange. However, this film is one of his films he wrote and directed himself.

This films an absolute masterpiece in everyway! The story is incredibly funny, but also extremely moving and sad. It also features arguably some of the best cinematography of all time! Most shots look as if they are a painting right out of the 18th century! While it is long, there is an intermission in the middle of the film, if you need a break or just want to finish it another day.

The film is 3 hours and 5 minutes, and is rated PG. While this film is rated PG it does contain some vulgar language, partial nudity, and violent deaths or acts. If this film were to come out today it would be rated PG-13.



Harakiri

Directed by Masaki Kobayashi and Written by Shinobu Hashimoto

Masaki Kobayashi is one of the greatest and most well Japanese filmmaker of his time! He created other classic films such as Kwaidan, Samurai Rebellion, and his most well known work The Human Condition series.

This film is incredibly well made and extremely well paced! The dialouge is absolutely fascinating, and the shots in this film are absolutely impeccable! It features an excellent fight scene with some of the coolest cinematography in a samurai film! The story however is what is truly special about this film. The intensity and constant twists keep you on your toes, and it truly stays that way till the credits roll. I can't recommend this film enough!

This film is 2 hours and 15 minutes, and is not rated. Beware this Film go in depth on very serious and heavy issues such as suicide and loss.



Watership Down

Directed and Written by Martin Rosen

Martin Rosen is an extremely underrated and lesser known director, who has not done too many films. His most notable film other than this one would be The Plague Dogs, which is another animated adaptation of a Richard Adams novel.

This film is one of my absolute favorite adaptations of one of my favorite novels! While this film differs strongly from the novel, it still gets most parts across, and almost makes it's own message and story in a way. The violence and the story is extremely jarring and intense throughout, but I think that's why it is so loved, and still looked at to this day. It features some beautifully drawn animation, and also features some stellar voice actors! I love this movie to death, and have ever since my first viewing.

This film is an hour and 42 minutes, and is PG. While, this film is rated PG, it does contain scenes of intense and violent deaths, along with intense and frightening scenes that may not be acceptable for children.
